Chhava: The Story of Sambhaji Maharaj

A Historical Action Film Based on the Life of the Second Chhatrapati of the Maratha Empire

Story

The film begins after the death of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, when Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb believes the Maratha Empire has weakened. However, Shivaji Maharaj's son, Sambhaji Maharaj, continues the struggle against the Mughals to fulfill his father's dream of Swarajya. Through his bravery and strategic brilliance, the Maratha army defeats the Mughals multiple times. Ultimately, internal betrayal leads to Sambhaji Maharaj's capture and brutal torture. The film highlights his sacrifice and unwavering commitment to Swarajya.

Direction and Screenplay

Directed by Laxman Utekar, known for films like "Luka Chuppi" and "Mimi," "Chhava" is based on the Marathi novel of the same name by Shivaji Sawant. The screenplay was co-written by Utekar, Rishi Virmani, Kaustubh Savarkar, Unman Bankar, and Onkar Mahajan. The story captures key events from Sambhaji Maharaj's life, showcasing his valor, strategy, and sacrifice.

Cast

Vicky Kaushal: As Sambhaji Maharaj, Vicky delivers a powerful performance with exceptional physicality, dialogue delivery, and emotional depth.
Rashmika Mandanna: Plays Maharani Yesubai, bringing strength and sensitivity to her role as Sambhaji's wife.
Akshay Kumar: Portrays Aurangzeb with calm authority, adding depth to the antagonist.

Music

The music, composed by A.R. Rahman, perfectly reflects the emotions and atmosphere of the story. Key songs include "Jaane Tu" (sung by Arijit Singh), "Aaya Re Toofaan" (by A.R. Rahman and Vaishali Samant), and "Zinda Rahe" (by Heeral Viradiya and A.R. Rahman). The background score enhances the film's emotional and dramatic scenes.

Box Office Performance

"Chhava" performed exceptionally well at the box office, earning ₹157 crore worldwide in its opening weekend. By the second Sunday, it had grossed ₹41 crore, making it one of Vicky Kaushal's biggest hits with a total collection of ₹242.25 crore so far.
