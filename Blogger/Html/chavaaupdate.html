Plot

The film's story begins after the death of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, when Mughal emperor Aurangzeb believes that the Maratha empire has weakened. However, Shivaji Maharaj's son, Sambhaji Maharaj, continues the struggle against the Mughals to fulfill his father's dream of Swarajya (self-rule).

Through Sambhaji Maharaj's bravery and strategic skills, the Maratha army defeats the Mughals on several occasions. But eventually, due to internal betrayal, Sambhaji Maharaj is captured by the Mughals and subjected to cruel torture. The film portrays his sacrifice and loyalty to Swarajya.

Direction and Screenplay

Laxman Utekar, known for films like 'Luka Chuppi' and 'Mimi', has directed 'Chhaava'. The film's screenplay is based on Shivaji Sawant's Marathi novel 'Chhaava', which Utekar co-wrote with Rishi Virmani, Kaustubh Savarkar, Unmesh Bankar, and Omkar Mahajan. The story depicts the major events of Sambhaji Maharaj's life, including his heroism, strategy, and sacrifice.

Vicky Kaushal has won the hearts of audiences with his performance as Sambhaji Maharaj. His body language, dialogue delivery, and emotional depth have brought the character to life. He underwent training in swordsmanship, horse riding, and the Marathi language for this role, which added authenticity to his performance.

As Maharani Yesubai, Rashmika Mandanna has portrayed a strong and sensitive wife. Her chemistry with Vicky is an important element in the film, providing emotional depth to the story.

The film's music has been composed by A.R. Rahman, accurately reflecting the emotions and atmosphere of the story. Irshad Kamil and Kshitij Patwardhan have added depth to the songs with their lyrics.

Cinematography and Technical Aspects

Saurabh Goswami's cinematography has captured the grandeur of the 17th century Maratha empire and the war scenes in a spectacular way. Manish Pradhan's editing has made the story flow smoothly, although some critics have given mixed reactions to the film's pace. The art direction, costumes, and production design have maintained the authenticity of that era, immersing viewers in that time period.
